What Happens Now

Closing day is done — but there are a few things that wrap up behind the scenes over the next few weeks. Here's exactly what to expect and when.

  • 1-3
    days

    Deed Recording

    The title company will record the deed with Greene County. Once recorded, you'll officially be out of the chain of title. This typically takes 1–3 business days.

  • 1-2
    wks

    Lender Payoff Confirmation

    If you had a mortgage, your lender will process the payoff. You'll receive a final statement confirming your loan is paid in full. Keep this document.

  • 2-4
    wks

    Escrow Refund

    If you had an escrow account for taxes and insurance, your lender will refund the remaining balance — usually within 20–30 business days of payoff. Watch for a check in the mail.

  • 30
    days

    County Tax Records Update

    Greene County will update their tax records to reflect the new owner. The transfer of responsibility for future tax bills happens automatically.

  • Jan
    next

    1099-S / Tax Documents

    You may receive a 1099-S from the closing. Share this with your tax professional — depending on your situation, some or all of your gain may be excluded. Ask your CPA about the primary residence exclusion ($250K single / $500K married).

  • 60
    days

    Closing Document Package

    You should receive your full closing document package from the title company. Store these in a safe place — you'll want them at tax time and for any future real estate transactions.

  • Now

    Update Your Address Everywhere

    This one's on you! Head to the Address Updates tab for a complete checklist — USPS, DMV, banks, employers, subscriptions and more.

Home Maintenance

A little maintenance now saves a lot of money later. Your seasonal checklists are here whenever you need them — and your maintenance log helps you track what's been done.

🌸 Spring Checklist

  • Inspect roof for winter damage — check flashing and gutters
  • Clean gutters and downspouts; check for clogs
  • Check exterior caulking and weather stripping
  • Service A/C unit — replace filter, check refrigerant
  • Test sprinkler system and irrigation heads
  • Inspect deck/patio for winter wear; clean and reseal if needed
  • Check driveway and walkways for cracks
  • Test outdoor GFCI outlets
  • Inspect window screens; repair or replace as needed
  • Deep clean the dryer vent (fire hazard if neglected!)

☀️ Summer Checklist

  • Check A/C filters monthly — replace every 60-90 days
  • Inspect and clean outdoor grills before each use
  • Check attic ventilation — proper airflow prevents heat buildup
  • Inspect plumbing for slow leaks under sinks
  • Trim trees and shrubs away from the house
  • Check crawl space / basement for moisture or water intrusion
  • Test smoke detectors and replace batteries
  • Inspect and clean range hood filters
  • Check exterior paint for peeling or bubbling
  • Flush water heater sediment (annually)

🍂 Fall Checklist

  • Service furnace / heat pump before first cold snap
  • Replace HVAC filters before heating season
  • Clean gutters after leaves fall — before first freeze
  • Drain and store garden hoses; turn off exterior spigots
  • Inspect chimney and fireplace if applicable
  • Check insulation in attic and crawl spaces
  • Seal gaps around doors and windows with weatherstripping
  • Test carbon monoxide detectors (more important with heat running)
  • Stock up on salt / sand for winter weather
  • Inspect garage door seals and opener safety features

❄️ Winter Checklist

  • Know how to shut off water main in case of burst pipes
  • Keep cabinet doors open in extreme cold (under-sink pipes)
  • Keep heat set to at least 55°F if leaving for extended periods
  • Check roof after heavy snowfall for ice dams
  • Keep walkways and driveway clear (slip & fall liability)
  • Test sump pump before spring thaw
  • Replace smoke and CO detector batteries (New Year's reminder)
  • Check for drafts around windows and doors; add temporary insulation
  • Run water regularly in rarely-used fixtures to prevent freezing
  • Document any issues for spring repair planning

Common Questions

Here are the questions I hear most from sellers after closing. And remember — if yours isn't here, just text me!

When will I get my escrow refund? ▼
Your lender typically processes the escrow refund within 20–30 business days after your loan payoff is received. It usually comes as a check mailed to your new address — another reason to get that address updated ASAP! If it's been more than 6 weeks, call your lender directly.
Do I owe taxes on the sale? ▼
Possibly, but most homeowners qualify for the IRS primary residence exclusion — up to $250,000 in gain if you're single, or $500,000 if married filing jointly. You must have lived in the home as your primary residence for at least 2 of the last 5 years. Talk to your CPA for your specific situation — I'm not a tax advisor, but I can absolutely refer you to a great one!
When will the deed be recorded? ▼
The title company submits the deed to Greene County for recording, usually within 1–3 business days of closing. Once recorded, it becomes public record. You can search the Greene County Auditor's website to confirm.
What happens to my homeowner's insurance? ▼
Your old policy on 2790 Pebble Stone Trail should be cancelled — but ONLY after you confirm the sale has closed and the deed has been transferred. Contact your insurance agent to cancel and request a pro-rated refund for the unused portion of your premium.
I got mail at my old address — what do I do? ▼
First, make sure your USPS mail forwarding is active at usps.com/move (takes 7–10 days to kick in). If it's junk mail, just leave it — the new owners will recycle it. If it's something important, they should write "Not At This Address" on it and drop it in a mailbox so USPS returns it to the sender.
